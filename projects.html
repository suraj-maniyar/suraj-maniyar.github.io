Projects

Measurement of Heartbeat of a patient using Infrared(IR) sensors

My final year project was titled "Measurement of the heart beat of an individual using infrared sensors."In this project, we developed a system which measured the heartbeat of a patient using infrared sensors and sent a distress message to a doctor's cell phone if the patient's vitals crossed the normal level.Working on this project ,I used my knowledge of advanced digital signal processing techniques such as Fourier analysis,statistical analysis and short-time Fourier transforms like Gabor Transform to effectively extract the essential information from input cardiac rhythms and designed filters to effectively reduce noise components from input signals.I also used my knowledge of integrated circuit fabrication and embedded programming to fabricate the circuit and to program the microcontroller respectively

Optimal Data forwarding in Wireless Sensor Networks

During the course of my undergraduate studies, I also worked closely with a group of researchers headed by Prof Dr Nandini Mukherjee at the 'School of Mobile Computing & Communication' at Jadavpur University.This group worked in developing novel ways of optimal data forwarding in wireless sensor networks(WSN).In this research project I devised a simulation which measured and compared the efficiency of routing data among 3 main wireless sensor network topologies-namely the Star,Cluster Tree and Mesh topology.While working in this research project, I learned about the embedded architechture of WSNs ,gained hands on experience in programming the WSN microcontroller using Moteview software and also gained valuable knowledge on the TinyOS operating systems.This research exposure provided me with the opportunity to understand how WSNs are widely used in remote monitoring applications like environmental monitoring of air,process monitoring and asset tracking.
